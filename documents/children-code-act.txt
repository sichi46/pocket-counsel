THE CHILDREN'S CODE ACT NO. 12 OF 2022

An Act to provide for the rights and welfare of children in Zambia, to establish mechanisms for child protection and welfare, and to provide for matters connected with or incidental to the foregoing.

PART I - PRELIMINARY

1. This Act may be cited as the Children's Code Act, 2022.

2. In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires—
"child" means a person below the age of eighteen years;
"child protection" means the prevention of and response to abuse, neglect, exploitation and violence affecting children;
"child welfare" means the promotion of the well-being of children through the provision of basic needs, care and support.

PART II - RIGHTS OF CHILDREN

3. Every child has the right to—
(a) life, survival and development;
(b) a name and nationality from birth;
(c) know and be cared for by his or her parents;
(d) not be separated from his or her parents against his or her will, except when competent authorities subject to judicial review determine, in accordance with applicable law and procedures, that such separation is necessary for the best interests of the child;
(e) maintain on a regular basis a personal relationship and direct contact with both parents, unless this is contrary to his or her best interests;
(f) express his or her views freely in all matters affecting the child, the views of the child being given due weight in accordance with the age and maturity of the child;
(g) freedom of expression, including the freedom to seek, receive and impart information and ideas of all kinds;
(h) freedom of thought, conscience and religion;
(i) freedom of association and peaceful assembly;
(j) not be subjected to arbitrary or unlawful interference with his or her privacy, family, home or correspondence;
(k) access to information and material from a diversity of national and international sources;
(l) education, including free and compulsory primary education;
(m) the highest attainable standard of health and to facilities for the treatment of illness and rehabilitation of health;
(n) benefit from social security, including social insurance;
(o) a standard of living adequate for the child's physical, mental, spiritual, moral and social development;
(p) rest and leisure, to engage in play and recreational activities appropriate to the age of the child and to participate freely in cultural life and the arts;
(q) protection from economic exploitation and from performing any work that is likely to be hazardous or to interfere with the child's education, or to be harmful to the child's health or physical, mental, spiritual, moral or social development;
(r) protection from all forms of sexual exploitation and sexual abuse;
(s) protection from all forms of physical or mental violence, injury or abuse, neglect or negligent treatment, maltreatment or exploitation, including sexual abuse;
(t) protection from all forms of discrimination.

PART III - CHILD PROTECTION

4. (1) No person shall—
(a) subject a child to torture or other cruel, inhuman or degrading treatment or punishment;
(b) subject a child to corporal punishment;
(c) subject a child to any form of violence, abuse or neglect;
(d) exploit a child sexually;
(e) use a child in armed conflict;
(f) recruit a child into armed forces or groups;
(g) use a child for begging;
(h) use a child for prostitution or pornography;
(i) traffic in children;
(j) sell or transfer a child for any purpose or in any form;
(k) use a child for the production and trafficking of drugs.

(2) Any person who contravenes subsection (1) commits an offence and is liable, upon conviction, to imprisonment for a term not exceeding ten years or to a fine not exceeding one hundred thousand penalty units, or to both.

PART IV - CHILD WELFARE

5. The Government shall—
(a) ensure that every child has access to basic health care services;
(b) provide free and compulsory primary education for every child;
(c) establish and maintain child care facilities;
(d) provide social assistance to children in need;
(e) promote the welfare of children with disabilities;
(f) protect children from harmful cultural practices;
(g) ensure that children in conflict with the law are treated in a manner consistent with the promotion of the child's sense of dignity and worth.

6. Every parent or guardian has the responsibility to—
(a) provide for the basic needs of the child;
(b) protect the child from harm;
(c) ensure the child receives education;
(d) provide the child with proper guidance and care;
(e) respect the child's rights and dignity.

PART V - ENFORCEMENT

7. (1) There is established a Child Protection Unit within the Zambia Police Service.

(2) The Child Protection Unit shall—
(a) investigate cases of child abuse, neglect and exploitation;
(b) provide protection to children at risk;
(c) work with other agencies to ensure the welfare of children;
(d) raise awareness on child protection issues.

8. Any person who has reasonable grounds to believe that a child is being abused, neglected or exploited shall report the matter to the police or a social welfare officer.

9. The Minister may make regulations for the better carrying out of the provisions of this Act.

This Act provides comprehensive protection for children in Zambia and establishes their fundamental rights including education, healthcare, protection from abuse and exploitation, and the right to participate in decisions affecting them.
